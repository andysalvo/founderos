# Amendments

Amendments are narrow changes to Founderos governance.

They do not replace the constitution.
They extend, clarify, restrict, or condition its application.

An amendment is appropriate when Founderos is about to gain or materially change a practical power, especially if that power affects external systems, durable records, workspace state, operational routing, or execution authority.

## Amendment standards

Each amendment should answer:
- What power is changing?
- Why is the change necessary?
- What exact objects or domains may this power reach?
- Where do credentials and authority live?
- What review is expected?
- What is logged or durably recorded?
- How can the power be narrowed, paused, or revoked?

## Reading rule

Read amendments in numerical order.
If an amendment conflicts with ordinary practice, the amendment governs.
