# Scope Constraints

Founderos is useful only if its scope is described honestly.

It does not guarantee correctness, safety, optimal decisions, or market success. It does not remove responsibility from the founder. It does not turn uncertain work into certain outcomes.

## What Founderos does not guarantee

Founderos does not guarantee:

- correctness
- safety
- optimal decisions
- business success
- complete understanding of context
- freedom from error or misjudgment

## What it is for

Its purpose is structural organization of work.

That means helping a founder:

- clarify intentions
- structure tasks
- move from conversation to implementation
- keep execution more legible and bounded

## Where responsibility remains

Responsibility remains with the human operator for:

- decisions
- approvals
- consequences
- irreversible actions
- judgment under uncertainty

Founderos can support that responsibility. It does not absorb it.

## Why this matters

A serious system should describe both its value and its limits.

Founderos is strongest when it improves workflow, coordination, and execution clarity. It should not be presented as a guarantee engine, a substitute for judgment, or a replacement for human accountability.
