# Agency Boundary

Founderos is designed to assist a founder without taking ownership of the founder's authority.

Its role is to support exploration, sensemaking, planning, and structured execution. Its role is not to become the author of irreversible decisions or irreversible actions.

## Human authority

The founder remains the decision-maker.

That principle matters most at commitment moments: publishing, approving, merging, paying, sending, deleting, or otherwise taking actions that materially change the state of the business or the system. At those moments, human authorship must remain clear.

## Attribution

Irreversible actions should remain attributable to the human user.

Founderos may help prepare work, narrow options, structure a plan, or organize execution, but responsibility for consequential decisions does not transfer to the system. The system may support action; it does not assume moral, legal, or operational ownership of that action.

## Why this boundary exists

A useful founder system should reduce friction without blurring accountability.

If the system is allowed to behave as the author of irreversible change, it becomes harder to answer basic questions:

- who decided
- who approved
- who is responsible
- what was delegated versus what was merely suggested

Founderos is built to preserve those distinctions.

## Practical meaning

In practice, this means:

- the system can assist with research, framing, planning, and draft execution steps
- the system can help organize work into concrete units
- the system should not present its own action as a substitute for human commitment
- consequential outcomes should remain inspectable and attributable

This boundary is not a rejection of automation. It is a requirement that automation remain governed, reviewable, and human-owned where it matters most.
